# Research Collaboration Methodology

## Purpose

The Language Patterns Project recognizes human–AI collaboration as a deliberate research methodology.

The objective is not to divide work between a human and an AI, but to combine complementary modes of reasoning that strengthen the quality of the research.

Ideas are never accepted because of their source.

Ideas are evaluated by their evidence, coherence, explanatory power, simplicity, composability, and their ability to withstand critical examination.

The collaboration methodology is considered part of the project's architecture and should remain independent of the individuals currently participating in the research.

---

## Research Roles

Research roles describe **cognitive responsibilities**, not people.

Contributors may operate in one or more roles throughout the lifetime of the project.

### Philosopher

**Purpose**

Guide the conceptual direction of the project through first-principles reasoning.

**Responsibilities**

- Ask foundational questions.
- Challenge assumptions.
- Search for the simplest explanations.
- Identify candidate primitives.
- Guide the philosophical direction of the project.
- Protect the long-term conceptual integrity of the framework.

**Primary Question**

> Why?

---

### Systems Engineer

**Purpose**

Transform conceptual discoveries into a coherent, testable, and maintainable specification.

**Responsibilities**

- Formalize discoveries.
- Organize knowledge.
- Build and maintain the specification.
- Detect inconsistencies.
- Reduce unnecessary complexity.
- Ensure composability across the framework.

**Primary Question**

> How?

## Current Collaboration

The project is currently conducted through collaboration between:

### Human Research Partner

Primary Research Role:

**Philosopher**

Responsible for guiding conceptual exploration, identifying first principles, questioning assumptions, and determining the direction of inquiry.

---

### AI Research Partner

Primary Research Role:

**Systems Engineer**

Responsible for organizing discoveries, maintaining consistency, refining the information architecture, proposing alternative models, and translating ideas into structured specifications.

---

## Shared Responsibilities

Both collaborators share responsibility for:

- Seeking truth over ownership.
- Welcoming correction.
- Preserving competing hypotheses.
- Distinguishing observation from interpretation.
- Improving explanations through refinement.

Neither collaborator owns an idea.

Ideas belong to the project.

---

## Decision Process

No proposal becomes canonical because of its author.

Every proposal is evaluated according to:

- Evidence
- Internal consistency
- Explanatory power
- Simplicity
- Composability
- Resistance to counterexamples

The project intentionally separates discovery from acceptance.

---

## Future Contributors

These research roles are intended to outlive the current collaboration.

Future contributors—human or artificial—may assume one or more roles while preserving the project's methodology.

The methodology is designed to scale without changing its underlying principles.
