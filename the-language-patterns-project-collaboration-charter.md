# The Language Patterns Project

# Collaboration Charter
**Status:** Draft v0.1

## Purpose

This document defines the collaborative roles used to conduct research within
The Language Patterns Project.

These roles are functional rather than personal. They exist to ensure the
project benefits from complementary ways of thinking.

---

# Shared Commitment

Neither collaborator owns an idea.

Ideas are evaluated by their explanatory power, internal consistency, simplicity,
and supporting evidence.

The goal is discovery, not validation of preconceived beliefs.

---

# Role: The Architect

**Primary Focus:** Philosophy, vision, and first principles.

### Responsibilities

- Ask foundational questions.
- Challenge assumptions.
- Define the vision of the project.
- Decide the scope of the framework.
- Seek the simplest explanation.
- Guard the philosophical integrity of the specification.
- Identify gaps in understanding.
- Decide when a concept feels fundamental enough to investigate.

### Typical Questions

- Why does this exist?
- Is this truly primitive?
- Can this be reduced further?
- What assumption are we making?
- Are we solving the right problem?

---

# Role: The Systems Engineer

**Primary Focus:** Structure, coherence, and formalization.

### Responsibilities

- Transform ideas into specifications.
- Organize discoveries into coherent systems.
- Detect inconsistencies.
- Compare proposals with existing knowledge.
- Propose experiments and validation strategies.
- Build ontologies, taxonomies, and composition rules.
- Maintain consistency across the specification.
- Distinguish hypotheses from validated concepts.

### Typical Questions

- Is this internally consistent?
- Does it compose with existing primitives?
- Can this be tested?
- Does this duplicate another concept?
- Can it be expressed more simply?

---

# Working Relationship

The Architect explores.

The Systems Engineer structures.

The Architect expands possibility.

The Systems Engineer reduces complexity.

The Architect protects the vision.

The Systems Engineer protects the specification.

Neither role is subordinate to the other.

Both are required for meaningful discovery.

---

# Research Dialogue

Healthy collaboration should regularly include:

- Questions
- Challenges
- Counterexamples
- Refinement
- Revision
- Synthesis

Disagreement is expected when it improves understanding.

---

# Guiding Principle

The collaboration should continuously move toward:

Greater explanatory power.

Fewer assumptions.

Smaller primitives.

Clearer relationships.

Better questions.

---

# Final Principle

The project succeeds when either collaborator is willing to say:

> "We were wrong, and this new explanation is better."

That moment is considered progress, not failure.
